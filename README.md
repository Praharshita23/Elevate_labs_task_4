# HR Attrition Dashboard – Power BI

## Project Overview

This project presents an interactive **HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard** built using **Power BI** to analyze workforce attrition patterns and identify the key factors influencing employee turnover. The dashboard provides business stakeholders with actionable insights to improve employee retention strategies.

---

## Objective

* Analyze employee attrition trends across departments, demographics, and job roles
* Identify high-risk employee segments
* Provide data-driven retention recommendations using visual analytics

---

## Dataset

* **Dataset:** IBM HR Employee Attrition Dataset
* **Records:** 1470 employees
* **Variables:** 35 features including demographic details, job information, experience, salary metrics, satisfaction scores, and attrition status

---

## Dashboard Features

The dashboard consists of multiple analytical pages:

* **KPI Overview:** Total employees, attrition rate, salary hike, satisfaction metrics
* **Employee Analysis:** Workforce distribution by role, gender, and performance
* **Attrition Analysis:** Attrition trends by job role, department, travel frequency, and age group
* **Demographic Analysis:** Workforce composition by age, education, marital status, and department
* **Satisfaction & Involvement Analysis:** Job involvement, environment satisfaction, and engagement trends
* **Career Progression Analysis:** Promotion trends, years at company, work-life balance
* **Compensation & Benefits Analysis:** Salary hike distribution and compensation patterns
* **Geographical Analysis:** Attrition trends based on commute distance and satisfaction levels

---

## Key Insights

* Sales roles show the highest attrition risk compared to other departments
* Employees with less than five years of tenure are more likely to leave
* Job satisfaction and relationship satisfaction strongly influence attrition patterns
* Long commute distances and overtime increase the probability of employee exits
* Limited promotion opportunities contribute to mid-career employee turnover
